Dataset — How data was created

Python Data Simulation

No real gym data was used. A synthetic Python dataset mirrors peak-hour behavior and capacity constraints.

How the dataset was built — step by step

Step 1 — Hour distribution. Each of the 1,000 visits was assigned an arrival hour (6am–9pm) with peak hours (5–8pm) weighted 3–4× higher than off-peak hours.

# Assign each visit a realistic arrival hour hours = np.random.choice( [6,7,8...21], n=1000, p=[0.03,0.04,0.06...0.11] # peak hours weighted higher )

Step 2 — Wait times via Normal distribution. Peak visits draw from N(10, 2.5) and off-peak visits from N(3, 1.5), with caps to keep values realistic.

wait_time = np.where( is_peak, np.random.normal(10, 2.5, n).clip(4, 18), # peak: avg 10 min np.random.normal(3, 1.5, n).clip(0, 8) # off-peak: avg 3 min )

Step 3 — Defect assignment. Peak hours have 40% unavailability, while off-peak drops to 10% for maintenance or broken equipment.

equipment_available = np.where( is_peak, np.random.choice([0,1], n, p=[0.40, 0.60]), # 40% unavailable at peak np.random.choice([0,1], n, p=[0.10, 0.90]) # 10% unavailable off-peak ) defect = 1 - equipment_available

Phase 3 — Analyze

Root Cause Analysis

Three analysis methods point to the same bottleneck: peak demand outpaces fixed equipment capacity.

Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram — cause categories

The fishbone groups contributing causes by category.
People
  • Overcrowding during 5–8pm rush
  • Members clustering around popular machines
  • No awareness of less-congested areas
Process
  • No equipment reservation or scheduling system
  • First-come-first-served creates bottlenecks
  • Manual check-in slows throughput
Machine / Equipment
  • Limited number of treadmills and benches
  • Broken equipment not flagged in real-time
  • Uneven distribution of popular vs unpopular equipment
Environment / Method
  • Peak hour congestion in free-weight zone
  • No visual availability signage on floor
  • Layout does not guide traffic flow efficiently

5 Whys — drilling to root cause

The 5 Whys traces the issue back to scheduling and visibility gaps.
WHY 1
Why do members experience long wait times?
Equipment is occupied and unavailable when they arrive
WHY 2
Why is equipment always occupied at the same time?
Too many members arrive in the same 3-hour window (5–8pm)
WHY 3
Why are all members arriving in the same window?
No scheduling system — members arrive whenever they can after work
WHY 4
Why is there no scheduling system?
The gym operates purely first-come-first-served with no demand management
WHY 5
Why has no demand management been implemented?
Root cause: no optimization or real-time tracking infrastructure exists
Root Cause Statement
Excessive wait times are caused by demand exceeding available equipment capacity during peak hours, combined with the absence of a structured system to manage equipment allocation.

Phase 4 — Improve

Lean Solutions & Future State

Four countermeasures target the wastes identified in Measure and Analyze.

01
Reservation System
App-based equipment booking
Members pre-book specific equipment slots up to 24 hours in advance via a mobile app. Eliminates blind arrival during peak hours. The system enforces time limits (e.g. 30 min per treadmill) and sends reminders 10 minutes before.
Reduces peak wait time by ~60% · Eliminates pure waiting waste
02
5S Layout Redesign
Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain
Reorganize equipment zones with clear floor markings, category signage, and logical flow paths. Remove rarely-used equipment to free space. Color-code zones (cardio / strength / flexibility). Visual management makes navigation instant.
Eliminates motion waste · Reduces search time from 5 min → <1 min
03
Poka-Yoke Display
Real-time availability screens
Large digital displays at the gym entrance and on the app show live equipment status (green = available, red = occupied, yellow = reserved). Members know before walking to a machine. Broken equipment flags automatically via sensor or staff tap.
Error-proofs equipment access · Eliminates defect & overprocessing waste
04
Load Balancing
Demand smoothing across hours
Off-peak discount pricing (5–10% off monthly fee) incentivizes members to shift visits to 10am–3pm. Capacity alerts push notifications when gym hits 70% capacity. AI suggests alternative time slots during booking flow based on predicted congestion.
Flattens the peak-hour curve · Improves all-day utilization

Standard operating procedures (SOPs)

SOP 1 — Check-in Process
Members scan QR or key fob. Desk staff only engage if scan fails. Peak-hour queue managed with stanchion system. Max 90-second check-in target.
SOP 2 — Equipment Usage
30-min time limits on cardio during peak. Members log end of use in app. Equipment flagged defective immediately via app tap or red card system.
SOP 3 — Staff Monitoring
Floor staff conduct 15-min zone sweeps during peak hours. Escalate equipment issues within 10 minutes. End-of-day digital handover report filed.
